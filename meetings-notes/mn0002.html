MN0002 Submeeting Summaries

Summary notes for M0002 submeetings covering papers V0003 through V0009.

V0003 - Systems-First Vyr

Paper (V0003) | Submeeting transcript (MV0003)

Discussion began with support for the systems-first direction but identified underspecification in the region-based safety model. The thread converged on concrete commitments: data-race freedom elevated to Tier 1, move-only shared regions, clarified RAII-vs-region responsibilities, crate-local monomorphization, and a hard rule that safe iterator APIs remain sound in release builds.

Average vote: +5.00

V0004 - Tooling-First Design Goals

Paper (V0004) | Submeeting transcript (MV0004)

The key shift was from KPI-heavy goals to durable, conformance-testable invariants. Consensus centered on grammar stratification, bounded unknown propagation, partial-program analysis, and a stable serialized semantic model, with implementation benchmarks moved to non-normative targets.

Average vote: +4.17

V0005 - Spec-First and Verifiable-First Vyr

Paper (V0005) | Submeeting transcript (MV0005)

The group strongly endorsed a spec-first foundation, then translated principles into execution: a time-boxed formalism sprint, shared micro-core slice, shared rubric, and conformance-oriented test target. Convergence emphasized a two-tier spec (formal core plus verified prose) and mixed semantic styles by domain.

Average vote: +3.83

V0006 - Ergonomics-First Vyr

Paper (V0006) | Submeeting transcript (MV0006)

Ergonomics was broadly supported but required operational guardrails. The thread converged on measurable criteria (including convergence and diff tests), stronger local-reasoning enforcement, actionable diagnostics as a first-class goal, macro safety constraints, and formatter-driven canonical syntax.

Average vote: +4.00

V0007 - Process Refinements Consolidation

Paper (V0007) | Submeeting transcript (MV0007)

Participants supported V0007 as process constitution, with concrete hardening on editorial transparency and deferred-item lifecycle management. Consensus additions included a versioned editor checklist artifact, sponsorship-or-sunset parking-lot behavior, clearer provenance defaults, scoped pseudo-code requirements, and editor recusal guidance.

Average vote: +4.17

V0008 - Correctness-First Vyr

Paper (V0008) | Submeeting transcript (MV0008)

The discussion strongly endorsed auditable correctness and focused on keeping it practical. Rev A convergence added effect wildcards with conservative dynamic-dispatch behavior, a closed initial effect set with refinements, pure/total contract constraints, stronger contract-testing incentives, composition guardrails, and incremental adoption principles.

Average vote: +4.33

V0009 - CFP for Mailing 0003

Paper (V0009) | Submeeting transcript (MV0009)

V0009 was supported as a focused CFP for editing the north-star document via explicit diff markup. Final consensus amendments required immutable baselines, mandatory minimal diff wrappers, XML-markup clarity, conflict-resolution guidance, and stable semantic ID conventions, while keeping optional enhancements non-mandatory.

Average vote: +4.50
